What is a bogan?

A manifesto for the loud, loyal, DIY, and proudly unbothered.

The short version

A bogan is often treated like a joke. Sometimes it is a joke — but it’s also a culture. A practical, noisy, funny, working-class-adjacent culture with a strong sense of identity.

House rule: We don’t punch down. We take the piss out of ourselves and punch up at snobbery.

The values

  • Loyalty: show up for your people.
  • Humour: use laughter as a coping mechanism and a greeting.
  • DIY: fix it, build it, patch it, give it a go.
  • No pretense: you can be rough and sentimental in the same body.

So what’s bogan.co.nz?

It’s the shop, sure — but it’s also the archive. The field guide. The in-jokes. The place where bogans (and bogan-adjacent humans) can feel seen and have a laugh.
